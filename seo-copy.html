01 — The Work

Researched, written, and tracked across two banners

This project covered brand pages and subcategory PLPs for Shoe Carnival and Shoe Station. Shoe Station pages were primarily net new; Shoe Carnival was a mix of net new and refreshes of existing copy that had not been updated for AI-era search.

Each page required keyword research, brand voice calibration for the specific banner, structured formatting for SEO and AI readability (H-tag headlines, bullets, sectioned content), and a publish record in the tracking system before it was considered done.

02 — Copy Examples

Live copy — structured for search and for AI crawlers

The format follows a consistent structure: an opening paragraph, H2-level thematic sections with bullet subcategories, a gender/category navigation block, a "why shop here" value section, and a closing CTA. This structure satisfies both traditional SEO signals and the entity-recognition patterns used by AI search engines.

04 — Tracking System

Every page logged. Every metric captured from publish date.

A tracking spreadsheet logs every page: brand name, page URL, which banner it lives on, the publish date, and a link to the source document. GA4 performance data was pulled and attached to create a pre/post baseline for each page.

The tracking system was built in parallel with the writing — not added retroactively. This means every page has a clean performance baseline from the day it went live.
